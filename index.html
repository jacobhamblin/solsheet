Marine — Class Features

Hit Die: d10 · Saves: STR & CON · Armor: All · Weapons: All

LVL 1 Combat Style
Choose a combat style at 1st level that defines your fighting approach.
LVL 1 Combat Momentum
You have a pool of Momentum Points (MP) equal to your Proficiency Bonus × 5. Spend MP to fuel special combat maneuvers. Recover all MP on a short or long rest.
LVL 2 Action Surge
Once per short rest, take one additional action on your turn.
LVL 3 Combat Doctrine
Choose your Combat Doctrine subclass: Demolitionist, Sniper, or Heavy Gunner.
LVL 4 Ability Score Improvement
Increase one ability score by 2, or two ability scores by 1 each (max 20). You may take a feat instead.
LVL 5 Extra Attack
When you take the Attack action, you can attack twice instead of once.
LVL 6 Tactical Reflexes
You have advantage on initiative rolls and cannot be surprised while conscious.
LVL 7 Combat Doctrine Feature
You gain an additional feature from your chosen Combat Doctrine subclass.
LVL 8 Ability Score Improvement
Increase one ability score by 2, or two ability scores by 1 each (max 20). You may take a feat instead.
LVL 9 Combat Resilience
When you take damage, you can use your reaction to reduce it by your Constitution modifier (minimum 1). Usable a number of times equal to your CON modifier per long rest.
LVL 11 Extra Attack (2)
When you take the Attack action, you can attack three times instead of twice.
LVL 13 Indomitable
Once per long rest, reroll a failed saving throw. You must use the new result.
LVL 18 Apex Soldier
Your attack rolls score a critical hit on a roll of 19 or 20.
LVL 20 War Machine Protocol
You become an unstoppable force of combat. You regain your Action Surge use at the start of every turn. Your MP maximum doubles and Combat Momentum maneuvers cost half as many MP (rounded down).
Engineer — Class Features

Hit Die: d6 · Saves: INT & CON · Key Ability: Intelligence

LVL 1 Field Engineer
You are proficient with Engineering tools and light armor. You have advantage on INT (Engineering) checks made to repair or modify equipment.
LVL 1 Fabrication Protocols & Rapid Repair
Fabrication Protocols — you have a pool of Tech Points (TP) that fuel your abilities. TP = your Engineer level + INT modifier. Recover all TP on a long rest.
Rapid Repair (1 TP) — bonus action, restore 1d6 + INT modifier HP to yourself or a creature you touch. Scales: +1d6 at levels 5, 9, 13, 17.
LVL 2 Drone Deployment
Deploy a combat/support drone (1 action). The drone acts on your initiative, obeys your commands, and has HP equal to your Engineer level × 2 + INT modifier. Repair a drone for 1 TP (restores 1d6 + INT HP). If destroyed, rebuild over a short rest.
LVL 3 Network Systems & Subclass
Network Systems — you can interface with electronic systems within 30 ft as a bonus action (no tools required).
Choose your Engineering Specialization: Mechanist, Systems Navigator, or Technical Savant.
LVL 5 Overclock
Spend 2 TP as a bonus action to take one additional action (Attack, Dash, Disengage, or Use Object) on your turn.
LVL 6 Efficient Systems
Rapid Repair and drone repair each cost 1 fewer TP (minimum 0). You also recover 1 TP at the start of each of your turns in combat.
LVL 9 Advanced Repair
Rapid Repair now restores 4d6 + INT HP. You can spend 2 TP to remove one condition (blinded, deafened, frightened, paralyzed, poisoned, or stunned) from a creature you touch.
LVL 11 Dual Deployment
You can have up to two drones active simultaneously. Each drone can receive independent commands on your turn (no action required).
LVL 13 System Override
Spend 3 TP as an action to seize control of any mechanical, electronic, or networked system within 60 ft. It obeys your commands for up to 1 minute (Concentration). While controlled, the system cannot be used against you.
LVL 18 Master Engineer
Your TP maximum increases by 5. Overclock no longer costs TP — you can use it freely once per turn. All repair abilities restore the maximum possible HP.
LVL 20 Apex Technologist
You can deploy up to three drones. At the start of combat you regain all expended TP. System Override requires no TP and has no duration limit.
Operator — Class Features

Hit Die: d8 · Saves: INT & CHA · CP = 5 + PB + INT mod

LVL 1 Tactical Command
You have a pool of Command Points (CP) equal to 5 + Proficiency Bonus + INT modifier. CP fuel your combat protocols. Recover all CP on a long rest; recover half (rounded down) on a short rest.
LVL 1 Battlefield Analysis & Weak Point Exploitation
Battlefield Analysis — you can use a bonus action to analyze a creature within 60 ft. You learn its AC and any damage immunities/resistances. Usable INT modifier times per long rest.
Weak Point Exploitation (1 CP) — when you or an ally hits a creature you have analyzed, add your INT modifier to the damage roll.
LVL 2 Tactical Forecast
At the start of combat, roll 2d20 and set the results aside as Forecast rolls. Before any creature makes an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw, you may replace that roll with one of your Forecast results (no action required). You lose unused Forecast rolls when combat ends.

Improves to 3d20 at level 10.

LVL 3 Combat Protocols & Subclass
Unlock CP-powered Combat Protocols — tactical commands issued to allies as a bonus action:
• Advance (1 CP) — one ally within 60 ft may immediately move up to half their speed.
• Focus Fire (1 CP) — one ally within 60 ft has advantage on their next attack roll this turn.
• Defend (1 CP) — one ally within 60 ft gains +2 AC until the start of your next turn.
Choose your Operator Subclass: Tactical Commander, Defensive Coordinator, or Strategic Controller.
LVL 5 Combat Stimulant
Spend 2 CP as a bonus action to grant one ally within 30 ft an extra attack on their next Attack action this turn. The ally also ignores difficult terrain until the end of their turn.
LVL 6 Efficient Command
All Combat Protocols cost 1 fewer CP (minimum 0). You recover 1 CP at the start of each of your turns during combat.
LVL 9 Advanced Tactics & Tactical Layering
Advanced Tactics — unlock two additional protocols:
• Suppressive Fire (2 CP) — one enemy within 60 ft has disadvantage on all attacks until the start of your next turn.
• Coordinated Strike (2 CP) — up to two allies within 60 ft may use their reaction to immediately make one weapon attack against a creature you designate.
Tactical Layering — you can issue two Combat Protocols per bonus action instead of one.
LVL 10 Protocol Scaling & Forecast Improvement
Your Combat Protocol effects scale with your INT modifier (e.g., Defend grants INT-mod AC instead of +2). Tactical Forecast now generates 3d20 Forecast rolls.
LVL 11 Rapid Coordination
You can now issue Combat Protocols as a free action (no bonus action cost) once per turn. The first Protocol each turn costs 0 CP.
LVL 13 Total Command
Spend 3 CP as an action to issue a Total Command order. All allies within 60 ft who can hear you may immediately take a reaction to move up to their speed and make one attack, all without using their actual reaction.
LVL 17 Command Mastery
Your CP maximum increases by your INT modifier. All allies within 60 ft gain a bonus to their attack rolls equal to half your INT modifier (rounded down) while you are conscious.
LVL 18 Master Strategist
You generate Tactical Forecast rolls at the start of every round (not just the start of combat). You may hold up to 5 Forecast results at a time.
LVL 20 Supreme Commander
All Combat Protocols cost 0 CP. Your CP pool refreshes fully at the start of each round of combat. Allies within 60 ft cannot be surprised and have advantage on all saving throws while you are conscious and not incapacitated.
Kineticist — Class Features

Hit Die: d6 · Saves: WIS & INT · Kinetic Save DC = 8 + PB + WIS mod

LVL 1 Kinetic Blast
Ranged attack (range 60 ft) dealing 1d10 force damage + WIS modifier. Uses your WIS modifier for the attack roll. Scales with level: 2d10 at 5, 3d10 at 11, 4d10 at 17.

Current damage die: track with level above.

LVL 1 Kinetic Shielding & Psionic Control
Kinetic Shielding — as a reaction when hit, reduce the damage by 1d10 + WIS modifier. Usable PB times per long rest.
Psionic Control — you can manipulate unattended objects weighing up to 10 lbs within 30 ft as a bonus action (no attack roll required).
LVL 2 Elemental Affinity
Choose a primary element: Fire, Water (Ice), or Air (Lightning). Your Kinetic Blast gains +1d6 damage of that element type. You gain resistance to that element's damage type.
LVL 3 Psionic Specialization
Choose your specialization path: Elemental Channeler, Force Adept, or Battlefield Shaper. Each unlocks unique psionic abilities at levels 3, 7, 11, and 15.
LVL 5 Enhanced Blast
Kinetic Blast damage increases to 2d10 force (plus the elemental 1d6 from Elemental Affinity).
LVL 6 Environmental Surge
While within 30 ft of a source of your chosen element (open flame, body of water, storm, etc.), each damage die of your Kinetic Blast deals +2 additional damage.
LVL 9 Telekinetic Grasp
As an action, target one creature within 60 ft. The creature makes a STR saving throw (DC = 8 + PB + WIS). On a failure, it is grappled and restrained until the start of your next turn. You can maintain this as a bonus action each turn (Concentration).
LVL 10 Elemental Infusion
You gain immunity (instead of resistance) to your chosen element's damage type. Additionally, once per turn when you hit with Kinetic Blast, you can impose a secondary effect based on your element:
Fire — target is set ablaze (1d6 fire, start of their turn, until extinguished).
Water/Ice — target's speed is halved until end of their next turn.
Air/Lightning — target cannot use reactions until end of their next turn.
LVL 11 Psionic Barrier & Enhanced Blast (3)
Psionic Barrier — as a reaction when you or an ally within 30 ft is hit, you can create a barrier that reduces the damage by 1d10 + WIS modifier. Usable PB times per long rest.
Enhanced Blast — Kinetic Blast damage increases to 3d10.
LVL 13 Environmental Mastery
You can create a 20-ft radius environmental zone of your element (bonus action, Concentration, 1 minute). Within this zone, allies treat the element as if always present for Environmental Surge. Enemies entering or starting their turn in the zone take 2d6 elemental damage (Kinetic Save DC halves).
LVL 15 Force Pulse
As an action, unleash a devastating 30-ft cone of psionic force. All creatures in the area take 6d6 force damage and are knocked prone (Kinetic Save DC for half and no knock prone). Usable once per long rest, or spend 4 Kinetic Shielding uses to use again.
LVL 17 Gravity Lens & Enhanced Blast (4)
Gravity Lens — as a bonus action, designate a point within 60 ft. Until the start of your next turn, all ranged attack rolls targeting creatures within 10 ft of that point have disadvantage, and all your Kinetic Blast attacks against creatures in that area have advantage.
Enhanced Blast — Kinetic Blast damage increases to 4d10.
LVL 20 Psionic Ascendance
Your psionic power reaches its apex. Kinetic Shielding recharges at the start of each of your turns (you always have uses available). Force Pulse recharges on a short or long rest. Your Kinetic Blast ignores resistance to force and elemental damage, and the range extends to 120 ft.
Specialist — Class Features

Hit Die: d8 · Saves: DEX & INT · Precision Strike scales with level

LVL 1 Precision Strike
Once per turn when you hit a creature with a weapon attack and you have advantage on the roll (or an ally is adjacent to the target), deal extra damage:
+1d6 at L1 · +2d6 at L5 · +3d6 at L9 · +4d6 at L13 · +5d6 at L17
LVL 1 Tactical Stealth & Close Quarters Training
Tactical Stealth — proficiency in Stealth. You can attempt to hide as a bonus action even when only lightly obscured.
Close Quarters Training — you do not suffer disadvantage when making ranged attacks against targets within 5 ft. You have advantage on attack rolls against targets that have not yet acted in combat.
LVL 2 Ghost Step
As a bonus action, choose one: Dash, Disengage, or Hide. If you Hide, attacks against you have disadvantage until the start of your next turn or until you attack.
LVL 3 Specialization
Choose your Specialization: Infiltrator, Saboteur, or Marksman. Each path unlocks unique abilities at levels 3, 9, 13, and 17.
LVL 5 Deadly Accuracy & Precision Strike +2d6
Deadly Accuracy — when you make an attack roll with advantage, treat a roll of 9 or lower on either die as a 10 for purposes of determining whether you hit.
Precision Strike increases to +2d6.
LVL 6 Field Mastery
Choose two skills in which you already have proficiency — your proficiency bonus is doubled for checks using those skills (expertise).
LVL 7 Slip Reflexes
When a creature misses you with a melee attack, you can use your reaction to move up to half your speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
LVL 9 Shadow Operative & Precision Strike +3d6
Shadow Operative — you can move through the space of any creature without spending extra movement. While hidden, your movement makes no sound.
Precision Strike increases to +3d6.
LVL 11 Double Tap
When you make a Precision Strike, you can immediately make one additional weapon attack against the same target as a bonus action (no action cost beyond the bonus action). This additional attack does not benefit from Precision Strike.
LVL 13 Weak Point Exploit & Precision Strike +4d6
Weak Point Exploit — your Precision Strike dice increase from d6 to d8 against targets that are surprised, restrained, grappled, or have not yet acted in combat.
Precision Strike increases to +4d6 (or +4d8 against weak-point targets).
LVL 15 Execution Protocol
Once per long rest, as an action, make a single attack against a creature that is surprised, unaware of you, or below half HP. On a hit, the creature must succeed on a CON saving throw (DC = 8 + PB + DEX mod) or be reduced to 0 HP. Creatures immune to critical hits are unaffected.
LVL 17 Phase Ghost & Precision Strike +5d6
Phase Ghost — once per turn when you would take damage, you can use your reaction to become incorporeal until the end of your turn. You gain resistance to all damage and can move through solid objects (ending inside one deals 5 force damage). Usable PB times per long rest.
Precision Strike increases to +5d6 (or +5d8 against weak-point targets).
LVL 18 Elusive Killer
No attack roll has advantage against you while you are not incapacitated. You can Hide as a free action (not a bonus action) once per turn.
LVL 20 Perfect Killer
Your Precision Strike applies to every attack on your turn (not just once per turn). Execution Protocol recharges on a short or long rest. You always act in the surprise round regardless of how combat begins.
